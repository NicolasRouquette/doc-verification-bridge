    An R-algebra A is formally etale if Œ©[A‚ÅÑR] and H¬π(L_{A/R}) both vanish. This is equivalent to the standard definition that "for every R-algebra B, every square-zero ideal I : Ideal B and f : A ‚Üí‚Çê[R] B ‚ß∏ I, there exists exactly one lift A ‚Üí‚Çê[R] B". An R-algebra A is √©tale if it is formally √©tale and of finite presentation.

    We show that the property extends onto nilpotent ideals, and that these properties are stable under R-algebra homomorphisms and compositions.

    We show that √©tale is stable under algebra isomorphisms, composition and localization at an element.
