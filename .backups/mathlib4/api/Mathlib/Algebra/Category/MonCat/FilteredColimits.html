    The forgetful functor from (commutative) (additive) monoids preserves filtered colimits. #

    Forgetful functors from algebraic categories usually don't preserve colimits. However, they tend to preserve filtered colimits.

    In this file, we start with a small filtered category J and a functor F : J ‚•§ MonCat. We then construct a monoid structure on the colimit of F ‚ãô forget MonCat (in Type), thereby showing that the forgetful functor forget MonCat preserves filtered colimits. Similarly for AddMonCat, CommMonCat and AddCommMonCat.
