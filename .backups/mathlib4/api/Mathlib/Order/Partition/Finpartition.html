    Finite partitions #

    In this file, we define finite partitions. A finpartition of a : Î± is a finite set of pairwise disjoint parts parts : Finset Î± which does not contain âŠ¥ and whose supremum is a.

    Finpartitions of a finset are at the heart of SzemerÃ©di's regularity lemma. They are also studied purely order theoretically in Sperner theory.

    Constructions #

    We provide many ways to build finpartitions:

    • Finpartition.ofErase: Builds a finpartition by erasing âŠ¥ for you.
    • Finpartition.ofSubset: Builds a finpartition from a subset of the parts of a previous finpartition.
    • Finpartition.empty: The empty finpartition of âŠ¥.
    • Finpartition.indiscrete: The indiscrete, aka trivial, aka pure, finpartition made of a single part.
    • Finpartition.discrete: The discrete finpartition of s : Finset Î± made of singletons.
    • Finpartition.bind: Puts together the finpartitions of the parts of a finpartition into a new finpartition.
    • Finpartition.extend: Extends a finpartition of a to a finpartition of a âŠ” b by adding b as a new part.
    • Finpartition.extendOfLE: Extends a finpartition of a to a finpartition of b when a â‰¤ b, by adding b \ a as a new part (if nonempty).
    • Finpartition.restrict: Restricts a finpartition of a to b where b â‰¤ a by intersecting each part with b.
    • Finpartition.combine: Combines a family of partitions of pairwise disjoint elements into a partition of their sup.
    • Finpartition.ofExistsUnique: Builds a finpartition from a collection of parts such that each element is in exactly one part.
    • Finpartition.ofSetoid: With Fintype Î±, constructs the finpartition of univ : Finset Î± induced by the equivalence classes of s : Setoid Î±.
    • Finpartition.atomise: Makes a finpartition of s : Finset Î± by breaking s along all finsets in F : Finset (Finset Î±). Two elements of s belong to the same part iff they belong to the same elements of F.

    Finpartition.indiscrete and Finpartition.bind together form the monadic structure of Finpartition.

    Implementation notes #

    Forbidding âŠ¥ as a part follows mathematical tradition and is a pragmatic choice concerning operations on Finpartition. Not caring about âŠ¥ being a part or not breaks extensionality (it's not because the parts of P and the parts of Q have the same elements that P = Q). Enforcing âŠ¥ to be a part makes Finpartition.bind uglier and doesn't rid us of the need of Finpartition.ofErase.
