    Basic properties of holors #

    Holors are indexed collections of tensor coefficients. Confusingly, they are often called tensors in physics and in the neural network community.

    A holor is simply a multidimensional array of values. The size of a holor is specified by a List ‚Ñï, whose length is called the dimension of the holor.

    The tensor product of x‚ÇÅ : Holor Œ± ds‚ÇÅ and x‚ÇÇ : Holor Œ± ds‚ÇÇ is the holor given by (x‚ÇÅ ‚äó x‚ÇÇ) (i‚ÇÅ ++ i‚ÇÇ) = x‚ÇÅ i‚ÇÅ * x‚ÇÇ i‚ÇÇ. A holor is "of rank at most 1" if it is a tensor product of one-dimensional holors. The CP rank of a holor x is the smallest N such that x is the sum of N holors of rank at most 1.

    Based on the tensor library found in https://www.isa-afp.org/entries/Deep_Learning.html
