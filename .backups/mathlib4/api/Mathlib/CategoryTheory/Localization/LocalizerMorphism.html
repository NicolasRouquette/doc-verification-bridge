    Morphisms of localizers #

    A morphism of localizers consists of a functor F : C‚ÇÅ ‚•§ C‚ÇÇ between two categories equipped with morphism properties W‚ÇÅ and W‚ÇÇ such that F sends morphisms in W‚ÇÅ to morphisms in W‚ÇÇ.

    If Œ¶ : LocalizerMorphism W‚ÇÅ W‚ÇÇ, and that L‚ÇÅ : C‚ÇÅ ‚•§ D‚ÇÅ and L‚ÇÇ : C‚ÇÇ ‚•§ D‚ÇÇ are localization functors for W‚ÇÅ and W‚ÇÇ, the induced functor D‚ÇÅ ‚•§ D‚ÇÇ is denoted Œ¶.localizedFunctor L‚ÇÅ L‚ÇÇ; we introduce the condition Œ¶.IsLocalizedEquivalence which expresses that this functor is an equivalence of categories. This condition is independent of the choice of the localized categories.
