    Transformations between oplax functors #

    Just as there are natural transformations between functors, there are transformations between oplax functors. The equality in the naturality condition of a natural transformation gets replaced by a specified 2-morphism. Now, there are three possible types of transformations (between oplax functors):

    • oplax natural transformations;
    • lax natural transformations;
    • strong natural transformations.

    These differ in the direction (and invertibility) of the 2-morphisms involved in the naturality condition.

    Main definitions #

    • Oplax.LaxTrans F G: lax transformations between oplax functors F and G. The naturality condition is given by a 2-morphism app a ‚â´ G.map f ‚ü∂ F.map f ‚â´ app b for each 1-morphism f : a ‚ü∂ b.
    • Oplax.OplaxTrans F G: oplax transformations between oplax functors F and G. The naturality condition is given by a 2-morphism F.map f ‚â´ app b ‚ü∂ app a ‚â´ G.map f for each 1-morphism f : a ‚ü∂ b.
    • Oplax.StrongTrans F G: strong transformations between oplax functors F and G. The naturality condition is given by a 2-isomorphism F.map f ‚â´ app b ‚âÖ app a ‚â´ G.map f for each 1-morphism f : a ‚ü∂ b.

    Using these, we define three (scoped) CategoryStruct instances on B ‚•§·µí·µñ·¥∏ C, in the Oplax.LaxTrans, Oplax.OplaxTrans, and Oplax.StrongTrans namespaces. The arrows in these CategoryStruct instances are given by lax transformations, oplax transformations, and strong transformations respectively.

    We also provide API for going between oplax transformations and strong transformations:

    • OplaxTrans.StrongCore Œ∑: a structure on an oplax transformation between oplax functors that promotes it to a strong transformation.
    • StrongTrans.mkOfOplax Œ∑ Œ∑': given an oplax transformation Œ∑ such that each component 2-morphism is an isomorphism, mkOfOplax gives the corresponding strong transformation.
