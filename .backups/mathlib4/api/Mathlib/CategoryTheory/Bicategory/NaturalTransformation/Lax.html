    Transformations between lax functors #

    Just as there are natural transformations between functors, there are transformations between lax functors. The equality in the naturality condition of a natural transformation gets replaced by a specified 2-morphism. Now, there are three possible types of transformations (between lax functors):

    • lax natural transformations;
    • oplax natural transformations;
    • strong natural transformations.

    These differ in the direction (and invertibility) of the 2-morphisms involved in the naturality condition.

    Main definitions #

    • Lax.LaxTrans F G: lax transformations between lax functors F and G. The naturality condition is given by a 2-morphism app a ‚â´ G.map f ‚ü∂ F.map f ‚â´ app b for each 1-morphism f : a ‚ü∂ b.
    • Lax.OplaxTrans F G: oplax transformations between lax functors F and G. The naturality condition is given by a 2-morphism F.map f ‚â´ app b ‚ü∂ app a ‚â´ G.map f for each 1-morphism f : a ‚ü∂ b.
    • Lax.StrongTrans F G: strong transformations between lax functors F and G. The naturality condition is given by a 2-isomorphism app a ‚â´ G.map f ‚âÖ F.map f ‚â´ app b for each 1-morphism f : a ‚ü∂ b.

    Using these, we define three (scoped) CategoryStruct instances on B ‚•§·¥∏ C, in the Lax.LaxTrans, Lax.OplaxTrans, and Lax.StrongTrans namespaces. The arrows in these CategoryStruct instances are given by lax transformations, oplax transformations, and strong transformations respectively.

    We also provide API for going between lax transformations and strong transformations:

    • LaxTrans.StrongCore Œ∑: a structure on a lax transformation between lax functors that promotes it to a strong transformation.
    • StrongTrans.mkOfLax Œ∑ Œ∑': given a lax transformation Œ∑ such that each component 2-morphism is an isomorphism, mkOfLax gives the corresponding strong transformation.

    References #

    • Niles Johnson, Donald Yau, 2-Dimensional Categories, section 4.2.
