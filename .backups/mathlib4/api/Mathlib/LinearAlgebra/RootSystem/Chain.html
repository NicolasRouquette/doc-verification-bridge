    Chains of roots #

    Given roots Œ± and Œ≤, the Œ±-chain through Œ≤ is the set of roots of the form Œ± + z ‚Ä¢ Œ≤ for an integer z. This is known as a "root chain" and also a "root string". For linearly independent roots in finite crystallographic root pairings, these chains are always unbroken, i.e., of the form: Œ≤ - q ‚Ä¢ Œ±, ..., Œ≤ - Œ±, Œ≤, Œ≤ + Œ±, ..., Œ≤ + p ‚Ä¢ Œ± for natural numbers p, q, and the length, p + q is at most 3.

    Main definitions / results: #

    • RootPairing.chainTopCoeff: the natural number p in the chain Œ≤ - q ‚Ä¢ Œ±, ..., Œ≤ - Œ±, Œ≤, Œ≤ + Œ±, ..., Œ≤ + p ‚Ä¢ Œ±
    • RootPairing.chainTopCoeff: the natural number q in the chain Œ≤ - q ‚Ä¢ Œ±, ..., Œ≤ - Œ±, Œ≤, Œ≤ + Œ±, ..., Œ≤ + p ‚Ä¢ Œ±
    • RootPairing.root_add_zsmul_mem_range_iff: every chain is an interval (aka unbroken).
    • RootPairing.chainBotCoeff_add_chainTopCoeff_le: every chain has length at most three.
