    Tensor product of an indexed family of modules over commutative semirings #

    We define the tensor product of an indexed family s : Œπ ‚Üí Type* of modules over commutative semirings. We denote this space by ‚®Ç[R] i, s i and define it as FreeAddMonoid (R √ó Œ† i, s i) quotiented by the appropriate equivalence relation. The treatment follows very closely that of the binary tensor product in Mathlib/LinearAlgebra/TensorProduct/Basic.lean.

    Main definitions #

    • PiTensorProduct R s with R a commutative semiring and s : Œπ ‚Üí Type* is the tensor product of all the s i's. This is denoted by ‚®Ç[R] i, s i.
    • tprod R f with f : Œ† i, s i is the tensor product of the vectors f i over all i : Œπ. This is bundled as a multilinear map from Œ† i, s i to ‚®Ç[R] i, s i.
    • liftAddHom constructs an AddMonoidHom from (‚®Ç[R] i, s i) to some space F from a function œÜ : (R √ó Œ† i, s i) ‚Üí F with the appropriate properties.
    • lift œÜ with œÜ : MultilinearMap R s E is the corresponding linear map (‚®Ç[R] i, s i) ‚Üí‚Çó[R] E. This is bundled as a linear equivalence.
    • PiTensorProduct.reindex e re-indexes the components of ‚®Ç[R] i : Œπ, M along e : Œπ ‚âÉ Œπ‚ÇÇ.
    • PiTensorProduct.tmulEquiv equivalence between a TensorProduct of PiTensorProducts and a single PiTensorProduct.

    Notation #

    • ‚®Ç[R] i, s i is defined as localized notation in scope TensorProduct.
    • ‚®Ç‚Çú[R] i, f i with f : ‚àÄ i, s i is defined globally as the tensor product of all the f i's.

    Implementation notes #

    • We define it via FreeAddMonoid (R √ó Œ† i, s i) with the R representing a "hidden" tensor factor, rather than FreeAddMonoid (Œ† i, s i) to ensure that, if Œπ is an empty type, the space is isomorphic to the base ring R.
    • We have not restricted the index type Œπ to be a Fintype, as nothing we do here strictly requires it. However, problems may arise in the case where Œπ is infinite; use at your own caution.
    • Instead of requiring DecidableEq Œπ as an argument to PiTensorProduct itself, we include it as an argument in the constructors of the relation. A decidability instance still has to come from somewhere due to the use of Function.update, but this hides it from the downstream user. See the implementation notes for MultilinearMap for an extended discussion of this choice.
