    Lagrange interpolation #

    Main definitions #

    • In everything that follows, s : Finset Œπ is a finite set of indices, with v : Œπ ‚Üí F an indexing of the field over some type. We call the image of v on s the interpolation nodes, though strictly unique nodes are only defined when v is injective on s.
    • Lagrange.basisDivisor x y, with x y : F. These are the normalised irreducible factors of the Lagrange basis polynomials. They evaluate to 1 at x and 0 at y when x and y are distinct.
    • Lagrange.basis v i with i : Œπ: the Lagrange basis polynomial that evaluates to 1 at v i and 0 at v j for i ‚â† j.
    • Lagrange.interpolate v r where r : Œπ ‚Üí F is a function from the fintype to the field: the Lagrange interpolant that evaluates to r i at x i for all i : Œπ. The r i are the values associated with the nodes x i.
