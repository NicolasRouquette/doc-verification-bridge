    §1 Escape Character Properties #

    The emitter's escapeChar function produces output that is valid for the scanner's collectDoubleQuotedLoopIx. Two properties:

    1. Characters that are escaped (e.g., \n, \\, \") produce valid two-character escape sequences recognized by processEscapeIx.
    2. Characters that pass through unchanged are nb-json characters that are neither " nor \.

    Both theorems are pure value-level; the indexed substrate plays no role here — they're verbatim twins of legacy §1.

      §3 collectDoubleQuotedLoopIx Acceptance — closure #

      State-dependent closure of §2.4. Imports the cursor-level surface correspondence (CursorSurfCorrIx), indexed advance helpers, state-dependent escape helpers, and the heavyweight core loop lemma.

      The cursor-level correspondence is a 3-field "lite" version of ScannerSurfCorrIx (without the indent-cols-nonneg field) — it matches IxCursor rather than ScannerStateIx, fitting collectDoubleQuotedLoopIx's cursor-centric signature. The state-level correspondence in ScanChain.lean §1.3 is the IxCursor + indent extension and decomposes to this one.
