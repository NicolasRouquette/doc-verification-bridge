    Hull-Kernel Topology #

    Let Œ± be a CompleteLattice and let T be a subset of Œ±. The pair of maps S ‚Üí sInf (Subtype.val '' S) and a ‚Üí T ‚Üì‚à© Ici a are often referred to as the kernel and the hull respectively. They form an antitone Galois connection between the subsets of T and Œ±. When Œ± can be generated from T by taking infs, this becomes a Galois insertion and the relative topology (Topology.lower) on T takes on a particularly simple form: the relative-open sets are exactly the sets of the form (hull T a)·∂ú for some a in Œ±. The topological closure coincides with the closure arising from the Galois insertion. For this reason the relative lower topology on T is often referred to as the "hull-kernel topology". The names "Jacobson topology" and "structure topology" also occur in the literature.

    Main statements #

    • PrimitiveSpectrum.isTopologicalBasis_relativeLower - the sets (hull a)·∂ú form a basis for the relative lower topology on T.
    • PrimitiveSpectrum.isOpen_iff - for a complete lattice, the sets (hull a)·∂ú are the relative topology.
    • PrimitiveSpectrum.gc - the kernel and the hull form a Galois connection
    • PrimitiveSpectrum.gi - when T generates Œ±, the Galois connection becomes an insertion.

    Implementation notes #

    The antitone Galois connection from Set T to Œ± is implemented as a monotone Galois connection between Set T to Œ±·µí·µà.

    Motivation #

    The motivating example for the study of a set T of prime elements which generate Œ± is the primitive spectrum of the lattice of M-ideals of a Banach space.
