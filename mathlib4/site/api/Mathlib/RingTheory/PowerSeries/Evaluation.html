    Evaluation of power series #

    Power series in one indeterminate are the particular case of multivariate power series, for the Unit type of indeterminates. This file provides a simpler syntax.

    Let R, S be types, with CommRing R, CommRing S. One assumes that IsTopologicalRing R and IsUniformAddGroup R, and that S is a complete and separated topological R-algebra, with IsLinearTopology S S, which means there is a basis of neighborhoods of 0 consisting of ideals.

    Given œÜ : R ‚Üí+* S, a : S, and f : MvPowerSeries œÉ R, PowerSeries.eval‚ÇÇ f œÜ a is the evaluation of the power series f at a. It f is (the coercion of) a polynomial, it coincides with the evaluation of that polynomial. Otherwise, it is defined by density from polynomials; its values are irrelevant unless œÜ is continuous and a is topologically nilpotent (a ^ n tends to 0 when n tends to infinity).

    For consistency with the case of multivariate power series, we define PowerSeries.HasEval as an abbrev to IsTopologicallyNilpotent.

    Under Continuous œÜ and HasEval a, the following lemmas furnish the properties of evaluation:

    • PowerSeries.eval‚ÇÇHom: the evaluation of multivariate power series, as a ring morphism,
    • PowerSeries.aeval: the evaluation map as an algebra morphism
    • PowerSeries.uniformContinuous_eval‚ÇÇ: uniform continuity of the evaluation
    • PowerSeries.continuous_eval‚ÇÇ: continuity of the evaluation
    • PowerSeries.eval‚ÇÇ_eq_tsum: the evaluation is given by the sum of its monomials, evaluated.

    We refer to the documentation of MvPowerSeries.eval‚ÇÇ for more details.
