    Chains of roots and weights #

    Given roots Œ± and Œ≤ of a Lie algebra, together with elements x in the Œ±-root space and y in the Œ≤-root space, it follows from the Leibniz identity that ‚ÅÖx, y‚ÅÜ is either zero or belongs to the Œ± + Œ≤-root space. Iterating this operation leads to the study of families of roots of the form k ‚Ä¢ Œ± + Œ≤. Such a family is known as the Œ±-chain through Œ≤ (or sometimes, the Œ±-string through Œ≤) and the study of the sum of the corresponding root spaces is an important technique.

    More generally if Œ± is a root and œá is a weight of a representation, it is useful to study the Œ±-chain through œá.

    We provide basic definitions and results to support Œ±-chain techniques in this file.

    • LieModule.exists‚ÇÇ_genWeightSpace_smul_add_eq_bot: given weights œá‚ÇÅ, œá‚ÇÇ if œá‚ÇÅ ‚â† 0, we can find p < 0 and q > 0 such that the weight spaces p ‚Ä¢ œá‚ÇÅ + œá‚ÇÇ and q ‚Ä¢ œá‚ÇÅ + œá‚ÇÇ are both trivial.
    • LieModule.genWeightSpaceChain: given weights œá‚ÇÅ, œá‚ÇÇ together with integers p and q, this is the sum of the weight spaces k ‚Ä¢ œá‚ÇÅ + œá‚ÇÇ for p < k < q.
    • LieModule.trace_toEnd_genWeightSpaceChain_eq_zero: given a root Œ± relative to a Cartan subalgebra H, there is a natural ideal corootSpace Œ± in H. This lemma states that this ideal acts by trace-zero endomorphisms on the sum of root spaces of any Œ±-chain, provided the weight spaces at the endpoints are both trivial.
    • LieModule.exists_forall_mem_corootSpace_smul_add_eq_zero: given a (potential) root Œ± relative to a Cartan subalgebra H, if we restrict to the ideal corootSpace Œ± of H, we may find an integral linear combination between Œ± and any weight œá of a representation.
