    Almost everywhere equal functions #

    We build a space of equivalence classes of functions, where two functions are treated as identical if they are almost everywhere equal. We form the set of equivalence classes under the relation of being almost everywhere equal, which is sometimes known as the L‚Å∞ space. To use this space as a basis for the L^p spaces and for the Bochner integral, we consider equivalence classes of strongly measurable functions (or, equivalently, of almost everywhere strongly measurable functions.)
