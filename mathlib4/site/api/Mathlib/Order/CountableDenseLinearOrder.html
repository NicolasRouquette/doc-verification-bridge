    The back and forth method and countable dense linear orders #

    Results #

    Suppose Œ± Œ≤ are linear orders, with Œ± countable and Œ≤ dense, nontrivial. Then there is an order embedding Œ± ‚Ü™ Œ≤. If in addition Œ± is dense, nonempty, without endpoints and Œ≤ is countable, without endpoints, then we can upgrade this to an order isomorphism Œ± ‚âÉ Œ≤.

    The idea for both results is to consider "partial isomorphisms", which identify a finite subset of Œ± with a finite subset of Œ≤, and prove that for any such partial isomorphism f and a : Œ±, we can extend f to include a in its domain.
