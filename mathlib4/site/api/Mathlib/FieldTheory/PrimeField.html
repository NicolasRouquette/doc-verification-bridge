    Prime fields #

    A prime field is a field that does not contain any nontrivial subfield. Prime fields are ‚Ñö in characteristic 0 and ZMod p in characteristic p with p a prime number. Any field K contains a unique prime field: it is the smallest field contained in K.

    Results #

    • The fields ‚Ñö and ZMod p are prime fields. These are stated as the instances that says that the corresponding Subfield type is a Subsingleton.

    • Subfield.bot_eq_of_zMod_algebra: the smallest subfield of a field of characteristic p is (the image of) ZMod p.
