    The radical of a quadratic form #

    We define the radical of a quadratic form. This is a standard construction if 2 is invertible in the coefficient ring, but is more fiddly otherwise. We follow the account in Chapter II, ¬ß7 of [elman-karpenko-merkurjev-2008].

    The radical of a quadratic form Q on M.

    This is the largest submodule N such that Q lifts to a quadratic form on M ‚ß∏ N; see Submodule.le_radical_iff for this characterization.

    See also [elman-karpenko-merkurjev-2008], Chapter II, ¬ß7.
