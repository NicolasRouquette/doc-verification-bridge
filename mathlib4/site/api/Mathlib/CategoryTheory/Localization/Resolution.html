    Resolutions for a morphism of localizers #

    Given a morphism of localizers Œ¶ : LocalizerMorphism W‚ÇÅ W‚ÇÇ (i.e. W‚ÇÅ and W‚ÇÇ are morphism properties on categories C‚ÇÅ and C‚ÇÇ, and we have a functor Œ¶.functor : C‚ÇÅ ‚•§ C‚ÇÇ which sends morphisms in W‚ÇÅ to morphisms in W‚ÇÇ), we introduce the notion of right resolutions of objects in C‚ÇÇ, for X‚ÇÇ : C‚ÇÇ. A right resolution consists of an object X‚ÇÅ : C‚ÇÅ and a morphism w : X‚ÇÇ ‚ü∂ Œ¶.functor.obj X‚ÇÅ that is in W‚ÇÇ. Then, the typeclass Œ¶.HasRightResolutions holds when any X‚ÇÇ : C‚ÇÇ has a right resolution.

    The type of right resolutions Œ¶.RightResolution X‚ÇÇ is endowed with a category structure.

    Similar definitions are done for left resolutions.
