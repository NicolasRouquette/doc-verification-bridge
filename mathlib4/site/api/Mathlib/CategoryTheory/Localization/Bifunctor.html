    Lifting of bifunctors #

    In this file, in the context of the localization of categories, we extend the notion of lifting of functors to the case of bifunctors. As the localization of categories behaves well with respect to finite products of categories (when the classes of morphisms contain identities), all the definitions for bifunctors C‚ÇÅ ‚•§ C‚ÇÇ ‚•§ E are obtained by reducing to the case of functors (C‚ÇÅ √ó C‚ÇÇ) ‚•§ E by using currying and uncurrying.

    Given morphism properties W‚ÇÅ : MorphismProperty C‚ÇÅ and W‚ÇÇ : MorphismProperty C‚ÇÇ, and a functor F : C‚ÇÅ ‚•§ C‚ÇÇ ‚•§ E, we define MorphismProperty.IsInvertedBy‚ÇÇ W‚ÇÅ W‚ÇÇ F as the condition that the functor uncurry.obj F : C‚ÇÅ √ó C‚ÇÇ ‚•§ E inverts W‚ÇÅ.prod W‚ÇÇ.

    If L‚ÇÅ : C‚ÇÅ ‚•§ D‚ÇÅ and L‚ÇÇ : C‚ÇÇ ‚•§ D‚ÇÇ are localization functors for W‚ÇÅ and W‚ÇÇ respectively, and F : C‚ÇÅ ‚•§ C‚ÇÇ ‚•§ E satisfies MorphismProperty.IsInvertedBy‚ÇÇ W‚ÇÅ W‚ÇÇ F, we introduce Localization.lift‚ÇÇ F hF L‚ÇÅ L‚ÇÇ : D‚ÇÅ ‚•§ D‚ÇÇ ‚•§ E which is a bifunctor which lifts F.
