    Points of a site #

    Let C be a category equipped with a Grothendieck topology J. In this file, we define the notion of point of the site (C, J), as a structure GrothendieckTopology.Point. Such a Œ¶ : J.Point consists in a functor Œ¶.fiber : C ‚•§ Type w such that the category Œ¶.fiber.Elements is cofiltered (and initially small) and such that if x : Œ¶.fiber.obj X and R is a covering sieve of X, then x belongs to the image of some y : Œ¶.fiber.obj Y by a morphism f : Y ‚ü∂ X which belongs to R. (This definition is essentially the definition of a fiber functor on a site from SGA 4 IV 6.3.)

    The fact that Œ¶.fiber.Elements·µí·µñ is filtered allows to define Œ¶.presheafFiber : (C·µí·µñ ‚•§ A) ‚•§ A by taking the filtering colimit of the evaluation functors at op X when (X : C, x : F.obj X) varies in Œ¶.fiber.Elements·µí·µñ. We define Œ¶.sheafFiber : Sheaf J A ‚•§ A as the restriction of Œ¶.presheafFiber to the full subcategory of sheaves.

    Under certain assumptions, we show that if A is concrete and P ‚ü∂ Q is a locally bijective morphism between presheaves, then the induced morphism on fibers is a bijection. It follows that not only Œ¶.sheafFiber : Sheaf J A ‚•§ A is the restriction of Œ¶.presheafFiber but it may also be thought as a localization of this functor with respect to the class of morphisms J.W. In particular, the fiber of a presheaf identifies to the fiber of its associated sheaf.

    Under suitable assumptions on the target category A, we show that both Œ¶.presheafFiber and Œ¶.sheafFiber commute with finite limits and with arbitrary colimits.
