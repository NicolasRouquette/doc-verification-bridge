    A Fubini theorem for categorical (co)limits #

    We prove that $lim_{J √ó K} G = lim_J (lim_K G(j, -))$ for a functor G : J √ó K ‚•§ C, when all the appropriate limits exist.

    We begin working with a functor F : J ‚•§ K ‚•§ C. We'll write G : J √ó K ‚•§ C for the associated "uncurried" functor.

    In the first part, given a coherent family D of limit cones over the functors F.obj j, and a cone c over G, we construct a cone over the cone points of D. We then show that if c is a limit cone, the constructed cone is also a limit cone.

    In the second part, we state the Fubini theorem in the setting where limits are provided by suitable HasLimit classes.

    We construct limitUncurryIsoLimitCompLim F : limit (uncurry.obj F) ‚âÖ limit (F ‚ãô lim) and give simp lemmas characterising it. For convenience, we also provide limitIsoLimitCurryCompLim G : limit G ‚âÖ limit ((curry.obj G) ‚ãô lim) in terms of the uncurried functor.

    All statements have their counterpart for colimits.
